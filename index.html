My journey at stakefish

I got to be exposed to the different projects and activities in the space. I especially related and dove deeper into a project called Solana and the NFT world. Understanding more about blockchain, both the technology and the philosophy behind it, had a big part in my will to understand better development and development logic.

The values that most resonate with me are transparency, decentralization, and financial literacy, and I try to reflect those values both in my career as well as my personal life. For example, I am a big believer in communicating clearly and matching expectations as part of being transparent. It can be at work, or with friends and family.

While working at stakefish for a couple of years, I also had the opportunity to be exposed to remote work and experience the advantages and challenges of being part of and operating in a remote-first company.
I got to meet and work with people from across the world. I got to be exposed to varied cultures, perspectives, and ways of communication. And with that, also understand the importance of collaborative and inclusive communication.

My journey at Abroadviser:

I started my own company, Abroadviser, for different reasons, but mainly it grew out of my challenges when I moved my life to a different country (South Korea) and the understanding that many like me might have experienced similar challenges.
I noticed that I am indeed bringing value to fellow newcomers by sharing my experiences and reliable information.
Seeing the impact it created around people I knew, I decided to start my own company and try my hand at growing a startup.

There were many lessons I have learned, from both business and operational perspectives. And I believe this experience was precious in growing myself professionally and personally.
I learned more about the importance of teamwork and adaptability among other things. Although my startup did not take off as I hoped it would, I could experience and be exposed to challenges I did not have before.
